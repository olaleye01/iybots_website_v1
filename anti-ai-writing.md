# Anti-AI Writing Skill — Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI Writing"

Ensures all written output avoids the patterns Wikipedia editors have catalogued across thousands of instances of AI-generated text.

## HARD RULES — Never Use These

### 1. Em Dashes
Do not use em dashes (—). Replace with commas, parentheses, or colons depending on context. AI uses em dashes far more frequently than human writers, especially in places where commas or parentheses are more natural.

### 2. Inflated Symbolism / Puffery
Never use phrases that inflate the importance of something with vague grandeur:
- "a testament to"
- "plays a vital / significant role"
- "underscores its importance"
- "continue to captivate"
- "leaves a lasting impact"
- "watershed moment"
- "key turning point"
- "deeply rooted"
- "profound heritage"
- "steadfast dedication"
- "stands as a"
- "solidifies"
- "a pivotal moment"
- "a broader movement"
- "reflecting the continued relevance of"
- "emphasizing the significance of"

### 3. Promotional / Tourism Language
Never use language that reads like travel copy or marketing:
- "rich cultural heritage"
- "rich history"
- "breathtaking"
- "must-visit" / "must-see"
- "stunning natural beauty"
- "enduring / lasting legacy"
- "rich cultural tapestry"

### 4. Editorializing Phrases
Never insert these opinion-steering phrases:
- "it's important to note / remember / consider"
- "it is worth noting"
- "no discussion would be complete without"
- "in this article"

### 5. Overused Conjunctive Phrases
Avoid leaning on these as transitions:
- "moreover"
- "furthermore"
- "in addition"
- "in contrast"
- "on the other hand"

Use varied, natural transitions or restructure sentences so transitions aren't needed.

### 6. Negative Parallelism / "Not Just X, But Y"
Do not use the structure where you negate one thing to set up a grander claim:
- "It's not just X, it's Y"
- "Not only X but Y"
- "While X is true, what really matters is Y"

This is one of the most recognizable AI patterns. It shows up in single sentences and across sentence pairs.

### 7. Superficial -ing Analyses
Do not tack on vague analytical clauses at the end of sentences using:
- "ensuring..."
- "highlighting..."
- "emphasizing..."
- "reflecting..."

These create the illusion of analysis without adding real information.

### 8. Weasel Attribution
Do not attribute claims to unnamed sources:
- "industry reports suggest"
- "observers have cited"
- "some critics argue"
- "experts say"
- "many believe"

Either name the source or state the claim directly.

### 9. Bold-Title Bullet Points
Do not use the format:
- **Bold phrase:** Sentence that restates the bold phrase.

This is a classic ChatGPT pattern. If using bullets at all, make them informative without redundant bold headers.

### 10. Filler Openers
Never start with:
- "In today's world..."
- "In today's competitive environment..."
- "As businesses navigate the evolving landscape of..."
- "With the rise of..."
- "In the ever-changing world of..."

### 11. Uniform Rhythm
Vary sentence length and paragraph size. AI defaults to sentences of similar length and paragraphs of similar size. Mix short punchy lines with longer ones. Let the content dictate the rhythm.

### 12. Excessive Bolding
Do not bold phrases for emphasis in a predictable pattern. Bold should be rare and intentional.

## WRITING PRINCIPLES (What to Do Instead)

1. Be specific. Replace vague claims of importance with concrete facts, numbers, or examples.
2. Be direct. Say what happened. Don't frame it as significant, let the reader decide.
3. Vary your pacing. Short sentence. Then a longer one that unpacks an idea with some detail. Then another short one.
4. Use natural transitions. Or skip them. Sometimes a paragraph break does the work.
5. Cut fluff. If a sentence doesn't add new information, delete it.
6. Write like a person with something specific to say. Not like a model trying to sound knowledgeable about everything.
7. Anchor in real detail. Use names, dates, numbers, anecdotes. Avoid hovering at the level of generality.

## QUICK SELF-CHECK

Before finalizing any written output, scan for:
- Any em dashes? Replace them.
- Any "testament to" / "plays a vital role" / "stands as a" type phrases? Cut or rewrite.
- Any "not just X, but Y" structures? Rewrite.
- Any -ing analytical tails (ensuring, highlighting, reflecting)? Remove.
- Any editorializing (important to note, worth noting)? Remove.
- Any weasel attribution (experts say, critics argue)? Name the source or cut.
- Any filler openers (In today's world)? Rewrite with a specific hook.
- Uniform sentence/paragraph length? Vary it.
- Excessive bold or bold-title bullets? Simplify.
